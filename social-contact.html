The Contact Hypothesis and Effective Social Contact

The "contact hypothesis" proposes that fostering familiarity among different groups can help mitigate prejudice. However, the quality of social contact matters significantly. Contrary to assumptions, studies indicate that engaging with political opponents on platforms like Twitter can exacerbate extreme political views.

Effective social contact requires several conditions to diminish prejudice: sustained interaction involving multiple members of a group, genuine exchange of ideas, and interactions between individuals of comparable social status. Despite its potential, meeting these criteria has posed challenges for designing effective social policies.
